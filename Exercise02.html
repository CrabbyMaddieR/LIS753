The Danger in the Stacks

Winterthur Poison Book Project: Deadly Bindings and Breathtaking Colors.

The Winterthur Poison Book Project is an ongoing investigation to explore the materiality of Victorian-era publishers' bindings, with a focus on the identification of potentially toxic pigments used as bookcloth colorants. This research came to life in 2019 and rocked the Antiquarian world.Nearly 50% of the 19th-century, cloth-case bindings analyzed for this project to date contain lead in the bookcloth. Analysis of a range of bookcloth colors has also identified arsenic, barium, chromium, copper, iron, and mercury.
